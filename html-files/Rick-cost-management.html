Risk & Cost Management

Understanding and Mitigating Threats in Your AI Journey

Threats to AI Success

Implementing AI in your enterprise brings transformative benefits—but it also introduces potential threats. Key risks include:

  • Cost Overruns: Gen AI cost estimates can be off by 500% to 1000%, creating significant budgeting challenges.
  • Security Risks: Without proper controls, AI systems may expose your organization to data breaches and other security threats.
  • Hallucinations: Inaccurate or misleading outputs from AI models can undermine decision-making and trust.

Cost Derailment Sources

It’s critical to understand where AI costs may spiral out of control. We categorize these into direct and indirect costs:

Direct Costs

  • Inference Costs
  • Data Preparation
  • Cost of Build
  • User Cost

Indirect Costs

  • Grounding Costs
  • Token Cost
  • Cost of I&O
  • Cost of Models

Effective cost management means monitoring these areas closely and leveraging strategies such as private, self-hosted LLMs to control expenses.

Mitigation Strategies

To ensure AI investments deliver value without financial derailment:

  • Monitor AI costs continuously, similar to cloud expenditure management.
  • Implement cost controls and performance tracking.
  • Consider self-hosted solutions to mitigate reliance on expensive, packaged AI products.
  • Integrate robust risk management frameworks such as TRiSM to secure your operations.

By understanding these cost drivers and threats, you can tailor your AI strategy to achieve sustainable outcomes.
